References

The HUGO Gene Nomenclature Committee (HGNC) is a committee of the Human Genome Organisation that sets standards for human genome nomenclature. An HGNC symbol is a unique abbreviation assigned to human genes to ensure clear and consistent communication in scientific research.

Tools

  • BioStars: A website about bioinformatics, computational genomics, and biological data analysis.
  • EBI Portal: The home for big data in biology.
  • Ensembl: A genome browser for vertebrate genomes that supports research in comparative genomics, evolution, sequence variation, and transcriptional regulation.
  • GeneCards: The human gene database.
  • HGNC: The resource for approved human gene nomenclature.
  • Rfam: A collection of RNA families, each represented by multiple sequence alignments, consensus secondary structures, and covariance models.
  • Xena: An online exploration tool for public and private, multi-omic and clinical/phenotype data.
